1 Types of Angles

  • Acute: 0° < θ < 90°
  • Right: θ = 90°
  • Obtuse: 90° < θ < 180°
  • Straight: θ = 180°
  • Reflex: 180° < θ < 360°
  • Full: θ = 360°
Important note: Always compare the angle measure with 90°, 180°, and 360° to identify its type quickly.

2 Types of Triangles

By sides:

  • Equilateral: all sides equal, all angles 60°
  • Isosceles: two sides equal
  • Scalene: all sides different

By angles:

  • Acute triangle: all angles acute
  • Right triangle: one 90° angle
  • Obtuse triangle: one obtuse angle
Important note: A triangle can be named by its sides and by its angles at the same time.

3 Trigonometric Identities

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ
sec θ = 1 / cos θ
csc θ = 1 / sin θ
Important note: These are the basic identity tools. Many trig problems become easier when you change everything to sin and cos first.

4 Pythagorean Identities

sin² θ + cos² θ = 1
1 + tan² θ = sec² θ
1 + cot² θ = csc² θ
Important note: The first identity is the main one. The other two come from dividing by cos² θ or sin² θ.

5 Right Triangle Trigonometry

sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
tan θ = opposite / adjacent
SOH-CAH-TOA
Important note: First choose the angle θ, then decide which sides are opposite, adjacent, and hypotenuse.

6 Even–Odd Identities

sin(-θ) = -sin θ
cos(-θ) = cos θ
tan(-θ) = -tan θ
sec(-θ) = sec θ
csc(-θ) = -csc θ
cot(-θ) = -cot θ
Important note: Cos and sec are even. Sin, tan, csc, and cot are odd.

7 Co-function Identities

sin(90° − θ) = cos θ
cos(90° − θ) = sin θ
tan(90° − θ) = cot θ
cot(90° − θ) = tan θ
sec(90° − θ) = csc θ
csc(90° − θ) = sec θ
Important note: These work because the two acute angles in a right triangle are complementary.

8 Trig Functions

  • sin θ
  • cos θ
  • tan θ
  • csc θ
  • sec θ
  • cot θ
Important note: Sin, cos, and tan are the main three. Csc, sec, and cot are their reciprocals.

10 Reference Angle

A reference angle is the positive acute angle between the terminal side of an angle and the x-axis.

  • Quadrant I: θ
  • Quadrant II: 180° − θ
  • Quadrant III: θ − 180°
  • Quadrant IV: 360° − θ
Important note: First find the quadrant, then use the correct formula for the reference angle.

11 Solve Trigonometric Equations

  1. Isolate the trig function.
  2. Find the basic angle.
  3. Use quadrants or the unit circle.
  4. Write all solutions in the interval asked for.
sin x = 1/2 → x = 30°, 150° on 0° ≤ x < 360°
cos x = -√2/2 → x = 135°, 225°
tan x = tan α → x = α + 180°k
Important note: Always check the interval. Some questions want one cycle only, others want general solutions.

12 Law of Sines

a / sin A = b / sin B = c / sin C

Use it when:

  • you know 2 angles and 1 side
  • you know 2 sides and a non-included angle
Important note: The side and its opposite angle must stay in the same fraction.

13 Law of Cosines

c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos C
a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A
b² = a² + c² − 2ac cos B

Use it when:

  • you know 3 sides
  • you know 2 sides and the included angle
Important note: This is the oblique-triangle version of the Pythagorean theorem.

14 Area of an Oblique Triangle

Area = 1/2 ab sin C
Area = 1/2 bc sin A
Area = 1/2 ac sin B
Important note: Use the two sides that meet at the included angle in the formula.

15 Heron’s Formula

s = (a + b + c) / 2
Area = √[s(s − a)(s − b)(s − c)]
Important note: Use Heron’s formula when you know all three sides and no angle.

16 Given ΔABC with sides a, b, and c: solve for the unknown values

  • Side a is opposite angle A
  • Side b is opposite angle B
  • Side c is opposite angle C
  • A + B + C = 180°

How to choose the method:

  • 3 sides: use Law of Cosines to find angles
  • 2 sides + included angle: use Law of Cosines
  • 2 angles + 1 side: use Law of Sines
  • 2 sides + non-included angle: usually use Law of Sines
Example: if a = 5, b = 7, C = 60°
c² = 5² + 7² − 2(5)(7)cos60° = 39
c = √39
Important note: Before solving, write what is known, what is unknown, and which formula matches that information.

17 Hard Examples by Question Style

1. Determine the value of the six trigonometric functions for ∠θ in the triangle

Example 1: In a right triangle, relative to angle θ, the opposite side is 9 and the hypotenuse is 15. Find all six trigonometric functions of θ.

adjacent = √(15² − 9²) = √144 = 12
sin θ = 9/15 = 3/5
cos θ = 12/15 = 4/5
tan θ = 9/12 = 3/4
csc θ = 5/3, sec θ = 5/4, cot θ = 4/3

Example 2: In a right triangle, relative to angle θ, the adjacent side is 7 and the hypotenuse is 25. Find all six trigonometric functions of θ.

opposite = √(25² − 7²) = √576 = 24
sin θ = 24/25
cos θ = 7/25
tan θ = 24/7
csc θ = 25/24, sec θ = 25/7, cot θ = 7/24

Example 3: In a right triangle, relative to angle θ, the opposite side is 12 and the adjacent side is 5. Find all six trigonometric functions of θ.

hypotenuse = √(12² + 5²) = √169 = 13
sin θ = 12/13
cos θ = 5/13
tan θ = 12/5
csc θ = 13/12, sec θ = 13/5, cot θ = 5/12

Reference: OpenStax – Right Triangle Trigonometry

2. Find the values of the six trigonometric functions of θ with the given constraint

Example 1: Given sin θ = 4/5 and θ is in Quadrant II, find the six trigonometric functions of θ.

sin θ = 4/5 → opposite = 4, hypotenuse = 5
adjacent = √(5² − 4²) = 3
In Quadrant II, cosine and tangent are negative
sin θ = 4/5, cos θ = -3/5, tan θ = -4/3
csc θ = 5/4, sec θ = -5/3, cot θ = -3/4

Example 2: Given cos θ = -3/8 and θ is in Quadrant III, find the six trigonometric functions of θ.

cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse = -3/8
opposite = √(8² − 3²) = √55
In Quadrant III, sine and cosine are negative, tangent is positive
sin θ = -√55/8
cos θ = -3/8
tan θ = √55/3
csc θ = -8/√55, sec θ = -8/3, cot θ = 3/√55

Example 3: Given tan θ = -5/12 and θ is in Quadrant IV, find the six trigonometric functions of θ.

tan θ = opposite/adjacent = -5/12
Use a 5-12-13 triangle
In Quadrant IV, sine is negative and cosine is positive
sin θ = -5/13
cos θ = 12/13
tan θ = -5/12
csc θ = -13/5, sec θ = 13/12, cot θ = -12/5

Reference: OpenStax – Trigonometric Ratios and Constraints

3. Solve Trigonometric Equations

Example 1: Solve on 0° ≤ x < 360°: 2sin²x - 3sin x + 1 = 0

Let y = sin x
2y² - 3y + 1 = 0
(2y - 1)(y - 1) = 0
sin x = 1/2 or sin x = 1
x = 30°, 150°, 90°

Example 2: Solve on 0° ≤ x < 360°: 2cos²x + cos x - 1 = 0

Let y = cos x
2y² + y - 1 = 0
(2y - 1)(y + 1) = 0
cos x = 1/2 or cos x = -1
x = 60°, 300°, 180°

Example 3: Solve on 0° ≤ x < 360°: tan(2x) = √3

tan θ = √3 at θ = 60° + 180°k
2x = 60° + 180°k
x = 30° + 90°k
On 0° ≤ x < 360°:
x = 30°, 120°, 210°, 300°

Reference: OpenStax – Solving Trigonometric Equations

4. Angle of elevation word problems

Example 1: An observer looks at the top of a tower at an angle of elevation of 28°. After moving 45 m closer, the angle becomes 41°. Find the height of the tower and the final distance from the tower.

Let x = final distance, h = height
tan 41° = h/x
tan 28° = h/(x + 45)
x tan 41° = (x + 45)tan 28°
x ≈ 40.81 m
h ≈ 35.48 m

Example 2: A person sees the top of a building at 35°. After walking 60 m closer, the angle becomes 52°. Find the height of the building and the new distance from the building.

Let x = final distance, h = height
tan 52° = h/x
tan 35° = h/(x + 60)
x tan 52° = (x + 60)tan 35°
x ≈ 77.53 m
h ≈ 99.23 m

Example 3: An observer measures the angle of elevation to the top of a cliff as 18°. After moving 80 m closer, the angle becomes 33°. Find the height of the cliff and the final distance.

Let x = final distance, h = height
tan 33° = h/x
tan 18° = h/(x + 80)
x tan 33° = (x + 80)tan 18°
x ≈ 79.27 m
h ≈ 51.47 m

Reference: OpenStax – Applications of Right Triangle Trigonometry

5. Given ΔABC with sides a, b, and c, solve for the unknown values

Example 1: Given a = 8, b = 11, and C = 47°, solve triangle ABC.

Use Law of Cosines: c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos C
c² = 8² + 11² − 2(8)(11)cos 47°
c ≈ 8.10
Use Law of Sines: sin A / 8 = sin 47° / 8.10
A ≈ 46.15°
B = 180° − 47° − 46.15° ≈ 86.85°

Example 2: Given a = 13, b = 17, and c = 21, solve triangle ABC.

Use Law of Cosines for A:
cos A = (b² + c² − a²) / 2bc
A ≈ 37.43°
Use Law of Cosines for B:
B ≈ 53.19°
C = 180° − 37.43° − 53.19° ≈ 89.38°

Example 3: Given A = 38°, a = 14, and b = 19, solve triangle ABC.

Use Law of Sines: sin B / 19 = sin 38° / 14
sin B ≈ 0.8358
B ≈ 56.73° or 123.27°
Case 1: C = 180° − 38° − 56.73° = 85.27°
Case 2: C = 180° − 38° − 123.27° = 18.73°
This is the ambiguous SSA case, so there are two possible triangles.

Reference: Khan Academy – Laws of Sines and Cosines Review
